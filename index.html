Common Questions

What is a Restricted Parking Zone (RPZ)?

An RPZ is an area where parking is controlled through permits and pay & display. Zone entry signs replace conventional double yellow lines. Within the zone, you can only park in marked bays with a valid permit or by paying at a machine.

How much will a permit cost?

Permit costs are based on your vehicle's CO2 emissions (or engine size for pre-2001 vehicles). Annual permits range from £80 to £220. Quarterly permits range from £20 to £55. Lower-emission vehicles pay less.

What are the operating hours?

The proposed scheme would operate from 8am to 10pm, 7 days a week. Outside these hours, parking would be unrestricted.

Can visitors still park?

Yes. Visitors can use pay and display spaces for up to 3 hours. Residents can also purchase visitor permits to allow guests to park for longer periods.

Does my email actually make a difference?

Yes! Each individual email is counted as a separate consultation response. Unlike petitions where hundreds of signatures are counted as just one submission, your personal email carries real weight. The more individual responses, the better the council understands community sentiment.

Who created this website?

This is an independent, resident-created resource to help the Dennistoun community participate in the consultation process. It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Glasgow City Council.
